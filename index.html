About Me

  • Name: Caden Carlson
  • Education: Wichita State University
  • Degree: Aerospace Engineering (In Progress)
  • Age: 19
  • Previous Employment: Carlson CAD, Grain Weevil, LEAAD Farms

For as long as I can remember I’ve been fascinated with the design and creation of machines that drive our world, most of all the ones that take to the sky or launch into orbit. I was drawn to pursue a degree in engineering because of this deep-set curiosity and will use it to make new, innovative strides in the aerospace industry. Almost without knowing it I began my career when I founded my additive manufacturing company Carlson CAD. Through this entrepreneurial endeavor, I’ve had the opportunity to serve clients across Nebraska in fields such as patent certification and marketing.

Beyond Carlson CAD, I’ve worked as a Research Engineer Intern at the Grain Weevil Corp., a company that uses robotics for grain bin management, and I participated in a National Science Foundation research grant as part of that team. Coming from a rural Nebraskan community I also worked on our family farm as a farm hand. Whether in the field or shop, this role had me performing hands-on tasks in often uncomfortable situations, yet it kindled my dedication to achieving real, actionable goals.

My time at Wichita State University is also filled due to my involvement as a Gore and Wallace Scholar and as an athlete on the track team.
